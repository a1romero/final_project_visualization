    California

    Investigating agriculture in California

    During our initial analysis, we noticed that California was a geographical outlier in terms of agricultural chemical usage. It has a relatively high chemical usage, but is located outside of the Corn Belt where most U.S. agriculture tends to take place. Here, we investigate whether California's geographic location influences the trends we've noticed for Midwestern farming states.

    Chemical usage in California agriculture

    California has most of its agricultural activity concentrated in the center of the state. The county with the most chemical usage is Fresno, in the central south. Other counties with high chemical usage are similarly situated. This could be because California has a desert to the southwest, and the coast to the southeast. Additionally, the gap between usage of insecticides and herbicides is much smaller than the average for the nation. It could be that California's climate is more hostile to weeds than typical farming climates, so they have less need for herbicide.

    Income and chemical usage in California

    California follows the trend we hypothesized; most counties with high levels of chemical usage have personal income per capita around or below the national average.
