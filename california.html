    California

    Investigating Agriculture in California

    In 2022, California had the largest agricultural output in the U.S. and generated $55.9 billion dollars in cash receipts. During our initial analysis, we noticed that California was a geographical outlier in terms of agricultural chemical usage. It uses high amounts of chemicals, but is located outside of the Midwest, where U.S. agricultural activity is concentrated. Because California contributes substantially to the U.S. agricultural industry, we conducted an analysis of agricultural chemical use and personal income data to see if the geographical differences between California and the Midwest impacted trends we had observed previously.

    Chemical Usage in California Agriculture

    California uses a higher proportion of insecticides, along with smaller differences in proportion between fertilizer and herbicides, than the rest of the nation. The counties marked with the most chemical use, like Fresno (3.4 million acres treated), Kern (2.8 million), and Tulare (2.0 million), all have a higher proportion of insecticide use compared to herbicide use. These counties may be responsible for the higher proportion of insecticide use compared to the nation.

    Alpine County is excluded from the dataset because it's missing chemical usage data. More information on our dataset can be found on our about page.

    The county with the most chemical usage is Fresno, located in the central south. The concentration of chemical usage is also in the central south. This could be because the southeast is a desert region, and the southwest is coastal.

    Income and Chemical Usage in California

    California follows the trend we hypothesized; most counties with high levels of chemical usage have personal income per capita around or below the national average ($50,155.84). All counties that treated more than 1.22 million acres with chemicals had personal incomes below the national average.

    Conclusion

    California offers an interesting perspective on U.S. agricultural trends. Due to its location, its chemical usage follows a very different distribution, where insecticide use is prioritized over herbicide use. However, California follows national trends very closely with respect to personal income and chemical usage.
