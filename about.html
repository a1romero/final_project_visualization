    The Data

    Chemical:

    This data is from the 2017 Census of Agriculture conducted by the United States Department of Agriculture and the National Agricultural Statistics Service since it is the most recent year. It can be found by using this query tool. We specified the commodity to be “AG LAND, ” with the measure being “TREATED, MEASURED IN ACRES.” For the state level visualization, we specified the geographic level to be “STATE,” while for our county level visualization we specified “COUNTY” and selected a subset of states. For the California visualization we only selected “CALIFORNIA.” We combined the fields “CHEMICAL, INSECTICIDE: (EXCL NEMATICIDES)” and “CHEMICAL, INSECTICIDE: (NEMATICIDES)” to create an aggregate “Pesticide” variable. We also chose to eliminate the fields called “FERTILIZER: (MANURE)” and “FERTILIZER: (ORGANIC),” because they are included in the field “FERTILIZER: (TOTAL).” We believe these fields should be included in the overall visualization because they can result in nutrient pollution if used in excess.

    Income:

    For the state level, real personal income for 2017 is denoted in this report made in 2018 by the Bureau of Economic Analysis. While there is a more recent year, we chose to look at 2017 for income so that this data would be consistent with our most recent chemical usage data. For the county level, we continued to use 2017 income data from the Bureau of Economic Analysis found in this report.
